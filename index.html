Where it all began ...


With design shifting towards web, as a print/graphic designer I was often tasked with designing for web. I was very fortunate that my company had enrolled me in a part-time web course at Shillington college geared towards graphic designers which a gave me better understanding of how the two work together. As a result of this, I was able to redesign a total of four company websites, including motoguzzi.com.au, the first website I coded from scratch myself.

Having background in design, I often find that I can draw upon my knowledge of user experience, along with design fundamentals and concept development to help plan out my web applications, so that they are not only functional but also user friendly.

Wireframe


Wireframes are meant to be simple, to let you focus on the informational hierarchy, the content arrangement and user experience, before adding all the aesthetics.

I wanted to build a drag and drop wireframing application for non-designers who aren't familiar with wireframing. And wanted to encourage the use of a 12-column-grid design system and rows, that will help stylise the layout and spacing on the page.
